What is molibdenum?

It is a metallic chemical element with atomic number 42, gray or black in color with a silvery shine, heavy, soft, and ductile in its pure state, but brittle if it contains impurities, scarce in the Earth's crust, where it is generally found in the form of sulfide, and used in the manufacture of steel and filaments resistant to high temperatures.
